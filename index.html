Large Synoptic Survey Telescope (LSST) Supernova (SNe) Visualization

About

This project was developed by Pavana Doddi, Ryan Nishimoto, and Anjali Yadla at the University of Illinois at Chicago as part of the Visual Data Science graduate course in Fall 2019. This project was developed in collaboration with Dr. Aaron Geller and Dr. Mark SubbaRao of the Adler Planetarium through an activity-centered-design process, with particular emphasis on the functionality of the system, and with repeated feedback from our client, course staff, and classmates

This Visualization looks at positional and categorical data of SNe over time of discovery. "Old" Data represents the current SNe that have been discovered from multiple sources, whereas the "LSST" Data represents simulated data the LSST is expected to find when it becomes operational

Controls:

Left Mouse: Rotate, Scroll: Zoom

Point Settings:

Size: Control size of SNe points

Attenuation: Toggle whether SNe point sizes change based on distance from the camera

Sprite: Toggle the use of a round sprite v.s. pixel representation of a SNe point

Additive Blending: Toggle whether SNe points become brighter when 'stacked' on top of one another

Unselected Opacity: Control how visible unselected SNe are

Disclaimers:

This Old data is not representative of the entire SNe Dataset - Data was cleaned and filtered to only include SNe that had enough positional data to plot, as well as focusing only specifically on Type I, Ia, and Type II SNe.

The LSST Data is not done being built at the time of this visualization, so the data used is simulated
